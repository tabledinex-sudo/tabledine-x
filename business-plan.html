TableDineX — Full Business Plan (Executive Summary + Details)

Executive Summary

TableDineX transforms restaurant tables into premium advertising surfaces and engagement zones — connecting brands directly to consumers at the point of experience. Our service enables restaurants to monetize table space via short, high-impact ad placements while brands gain attention in a captive, high-intent environment.

  • Business model: Revenue share with restaurants + campaign fees from brands + SaaS subscriptions for analytics.
  • Core offering: Table-top ad placements (print, laminated cards, QR-enabled interactive cards) and a SaaS portal for brands to book, upload creatives, and track campaign performance.
  • Market focus: Urban restaurants, cafes, and QSRs in India initially; expand to additional cities and countries.

Vision, Mission & Values

Vision: To make every dining experience an opportunity for brands to build memorable interactions globally.

Mission: To deliver measurable, high-impact, and affordable table-level advertising that benefits restaurants, delights diners, and drives ROI for brands.

Core values: Partnership-first, measurable outcomes, creative-first, data-driven, and ethical.

Problem Statement

Restaurants have underutilized physical assets (table space) while brands struggle to reach consumers in moment-driven contexts with measurable ROI. Digital ads have high reach but lower attention; influencer campaigns are expensive. Brands need a high-attention, low-cost, localizable channel with clear metrics.

Market Opportunity (TAM / SAM / SOM)

We estimate a large opportunity across metro and tier-1 cities. Each restaurant has dozens to hundreds of table-sittings daily — creating millions of impressions per day potential. Appendix F contains a detailed spreadsheet model (available on request).

Product & Service Overview

Core Components:

  • Physical ad mediums: table tents, laminated placemats, coasters, NFC tags.
  • Interactive features: QR codes, short links, promo codes, AR markers.
  • SaaS Campaign Portal: For booking campaigns, uploading creatives, and viewing analytics.
  • Restaurant Partner Web App: Simple interface for restaurant managers to confirm placements and view payouts.

Advertising Formats & Creatives

Formats include table tents, placemats, coasters, stickers, and NFC tags. Creatives are optimized for table distance and quick CTAs (3–6 word headlines, clear CTA, QR + short-link, promo code).

Technology Platform

Frontend (React), Backend (Node.js/Python), Analytics (event tracking for QR scans and short-link hits), Hosting (Vercel for frontends, cloud backend on AWS/GCP). Security and privacy measures will be in place for handling any user data.

Value Proposition & Differentiation

For restaurants: additional revenue, minimal effort, monthly payouts. For brands: captive audience, measurable interactions, and cost-effective CPM. TableDineX is differentiated by measurement, simple restaurant onboarding, and creative-first templates.

Business Model & Revenue Streams

  • Campaign Fees (brands)
  • Revenue Share with Restaurants
  • SaaS Subscriptions for advanced analytics
  • Creative Services and Premium Installations (NFC/table devices)

Sales & Partnership Strategy

Onboard restaurants via city managers and sales reps; offer sign-up incentives and pilot guarantees. For brands, run pilots, gather case studies, and expand through agencies and resellers.

Operations Plan

Local production partners produce physical materials; field teams or vendors install materials. Monthly audits and QR health checks will ensure campaign quality.

Marketing & Growth Strategy

Pilot in 3 cities, gather case studies, and scale to more cities. Mix digital outreach to brands with local events for restaurants.

Financials & Unit Economics (Summary)

Key metrics include revenue per table per month, gross margin per table, CAC for restaurants/brands, and lifetime value of restaurant partnerships. A downloadable financial model is available on request.

Risks & Mitigation

  • Low restaurant uptake — mitigate with revenue guarantees and low friction onboarding.
  • Creative fatigue — mitigate with rotating campaigns and A/B testing.
  • Measurement inaccuracies — mitigate with unique promo codes and audits.

Roadmap & Milestones

Month 0–3: MVP and pilot. Month 4–12: city rollouts and pricing refinement. Month 12–24: expand to more cities and add premium offerings. Month 24+: international expansion and partnerships.
